Cayuga Lake: Established ~550 million years ago.
Cayuga Lake averages approximately 36 inches of rain and 66 inches of snow per year. There are 162 sunny days on average for the lake. The temperature varies from about 81°F in the summer and as low as 16°F in the Winter.
Cayuga Lake is one of the 11 Finger Lakes in Upstate New York. At 37.9 miles long, Cayuga is the longest lake, closely followed by Seneca. Up to ~435 feet deep, it is only second deepest to its neighbor, Seneca lake. At the North end of the lake, we have Montezuma National Wildlife Refuge, and at the bottom is Ithaca, NY. The surrounding area features many wineries and farms, includint Amish and Mennonite populations. There are two yacht clubs on the lake; the southern one- Ithaca Yacht Club- and the northern one- Red Jacket Yacht Club.
Cayuga Lake:
-Cayuga Lake is 37.9 miles long.
-It is a glacial lake formed by a glacier many years ago.
-Widest point is 3.5 miles long, near Aurora.
-The Finger Lakes are said to be carved by glaciers during the last ice age.
-Cayuga is connected to Seneca Lake by the Seneca River and to Lake Ontario by the Erie Canal.
-Includes Boy Scout Camp Barton at Frontenac Point on the Western Shore.
-Also along the Western Shore is Taughannock Falls State Park, featuring a 215 foot waterfall; the tallest single-drop waterfall east of the Rocky Mountains.
